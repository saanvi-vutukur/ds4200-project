Enterprise Adoption of AI Tools

The rapid adoption of generative AI tools across industries has raised pressing questions about the uniformity of their impact. Although tools like ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini are broadly accessible, the extent to which firms integrate these technologies — and the outcomes they experience — varies significantly by sector. Industries such as Finance and Technology may invest in different tools, commit varying levels of training, and see distinct workforce outcomes compared to sectors like Healthcare. Understanding these differences is essential for business leaders and policymakers seeking to maximize the benefits of GenAI while addressing disparities in productivity and workforce impact. This analysis draws on a filtered subset of over 7,000 US-based enterprise observations spanning 14 industries. Through five visualizations, we examine productivity differences across sectors, workforce outcome distributions, and how company size shapes the return on AI training investment.

Saanvi Vutukur, Maya Karintholil, Aashka Dave

The data

Data description

Dataset: Enterprise GenAI Adoption and Workforce Impact (Kaggle). Each row represents a single enterprise's self-reported survey response. The original dataset contains 100,000 observations across 10 columns; we filtered to US-based enterprises only, yielding approximately 7,000 rows spanning 14 industries.

Key Columns: Industry, Company Size, GenAI Tool used, Adoption Year, Number of Employees Impacted, New Roles Created, Training Hours Provided, Productivity Change (%), and Employee Sentiment. We engineered three additional features: Years Since Adoption, Training Hours per Employee, and Training ROI. This brings the total to 13 columns with a strong mix of categorical and continuous variables.

Limitations: The dataset is survey-based, so productivity change figures are self-reported and may reflect perceived rather than objectively measured outcomes. The dataset does not distinguish between tool usage intensity (occasional vs. deep integration), and the US-only filter, while intentional, limits generalizability. Some industries have slightly unequal sample sizes, which may affect cross-sector comparisons.

Visualizations

Figure 1 · Distribution of Productivity Changes by Industry
This figure shows the full distribution of productivity change (%) across industries using a boxplot. Unlike a simple average-based chart, the boxplot highlights median values, interquartile ranges, and outliers, making it easier to compare both central tendency and variability across sectors. Industries such as Technology and Retail show relatively higher medians and tighter distributions, suggesting more consistent productivity gains, while sectors like Healthcare and Finance show wider spreads, indicating more uneven outcomes across firms. Overall, the visualization emphasizes that GenAI impact is not only different in magnitude across industries but also in consistency of outcomes within industries.

Figure 2 · Productivity Impact with Standard Error
Industries like Technology and Retail not only have higher average productivity gains, but their confidence intervals do not overlap with those of lower-performing sectors like Entertainment and Healthcare. This suggests that these differences are statistically meaningful. Conversely, industries such as Hospitality, Education, and Legal Services show wide error bars, indicating high variability in enterprise experiences within those sectors. This suggests that for some industries, the benefit of GenAI adoption depends heavily on firm-specific factors such as implementation quality or workforce readiness.

Figure 3 · Hours Training vs Productivity with ROI Insight
This multivariate scatterplot explores the relationship between training investment and productivity outcomes across enterprises. The x-axis represents Training Hours per Employee, while the y-axis shows Productivity Change (%), allowing direct assessment of whether higher training investment is associated with improved performance. Each point represents a firm, and color encodes industry, enabling cross-sector comparison within the same space. Point size reflects Training ROI, adding a third dimension that highlights how efficiently training translates into returns. The visualization shows a weak but positive relationship overall, with significant variation across industries, suggesting that training effectiveness depends more on implementation quality and industry context than on training volume alone.

Figure 4 · Training ROI by Company Size
This grouped bar chart compares average Training ROI across company sizes within each industry. Instead of aggregating values, the visualization keeps each company size separate to allow direct comparison at the same scale. The results show a clear monotonic trend: enterprise firms consistently achieve the highest training ROI, followed by large, medium, and small companies. This pattern holds across most industries, although the magnitude varies slightly depending on sector. The interactive industry filter allows users to isolate specific sectors and verify whether the size effect is consistent or driven by particular industries.

Figure 5 · Industry × Workforce Impact Heatmap
This interactive heatmap allows direct comparison of how GenAI adoption outcomes are distributed across all 14 industries simultaneously. Each cell shows either the raw count or the percentage of enterprises in that industry reporting a given workforce impact. "No Clear Impact" is consistently the largest category everywhere, but the relative balance between "Reskilling & Growth" and "Role Change" varies by sector. Manufacturing and Transportation show relatively higher Reskilling & Growth shares, while Finance and Hospitality show comparatively higher Job Loss Risk. The sort control lets users reorder industries by any impact category to quickly surface which sectors are most exposed to workforce disruption.

Summary & findings

Unequal Productivity Gains Across Sectors

Productivity gains from GenAI are real but unequal. Technology, Retail, and Telecom see the strongest average improvements, while Entertainment, Healthcare, and Finance lag behind. This suggests that sector-specific barriers (regulatory constraints, workflow complexity, or data sensitivity) moderate how much productivity value enterprises extract from these tools.

Reskilling Dominates Over Job Loss

The dominant workforce outcome of GenAI adoption is not displacement but evolution. "No Major Impact" and "Reskilling & Growth" together account for the vast majority of outcomes in every industry. Job Loss Risk is present but consistently the smallest category, countering the most alarming narratives around AI and employment.

Size Drives Training ROI

Company size is the single strongest predictor of Training ROI. Enterprise-scale organizations extract nearly 15 times the training return of small companies. This gap suggests that policymakers and smaller firms should focus on scaling training programs and sharing best practices to close the size-based advantage gap.

Future work

Future work could examine how these patterns shift as adoption matures beyond 2024, explore differences between specific GenAI tools (e.g., ChatGPT vs. Copilot), or incorporate objective productivity measures rather than self-reported estimates.
